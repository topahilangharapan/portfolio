The phrase that stopped me in DD Consulting's Fellowship description wasn't the salary
or the certification subsidy --- it was \textit{Building AI using AI}. I'd already been
living that at Traveloka: embedding GitHub Copilot CLI into our backend squad's workflow
didn't just accelerate delivery, it changed how the team reasoned about automation itself.
That hands-on experience with agentic tooling, combined with a genuine interest in how
AI systems are governed and audited, is what draws me to this Fellowship and to the
multi-disciplinary model DD Consulting has built around it.

% ============================================================
% P2 -- PROOF
% ============================================================
At Traveloka, one of the largest flight inventory providers had no automated reschedule
path --- operators manually handled thousands of daily booking modifications. I built an
end-to-end agentic pipeline with configurable scrapers, failover backup mechanisms, and
99.9\% processing accuracy, cutting manual intervention by 85\%. The governance half was
equally deliberate: I engineered a Credential Management System with AES-GCM encryption
and granular audit logging, because autonomous workflows that can't be traced or
constrained aren't productivity tools --- they're liabilities. Those two projects, taken
together, are a close analogue to what the Fellowship asks for: autonomous system
implementation paired with the accountability layer to make it safe to operate.

% ============================================================
% P3 -- FIT
% ============================================================
DD Consulting's specialisation in auditing AI systems against ISO 42001 sits at exactly
the intersection I want to build expertise in. I've been running on GCP throughout my
time at Traveloka --- BigQuery pipelines, containerised deployments, cloud-native CI/CD
--- and I'm actively working through the AWS Solutions Architect path, so the cloud
infrastructure side of the Fellowship is ground I'm already covering. What excites me
most is the consulting dimension: translating complex multi-agent architectures into
something a non-technical stakeholder can understand, trust, and sign off on. That's a
skill I started developing as a mentor at Fasilkom and as a cross-functional liaison
between engineering and design divisions at COMPFEST.

the transition from hands-on development to governance consulting --- and whether the
